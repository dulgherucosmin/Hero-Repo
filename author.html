R.F. Kuang's Life

Rebecca F. Kuang, commonly known as R.F. Kuang, is a Chinese-American writer and scholar. Born on May 29, 1996, in Guangzhou, China, she immigrated to the United States with her family when she was four years old. She grew up in Dallas, Texas, where she developed a deep passion for history, literature, and languages.

Kuang’s interest in storytelling and historical narratives was shaped by her early exposure to both Western and Chinese literature. As she grew older, she became particularly fascinated by the lasting effects of war, colonialism, and cultural identity—topics that would later define much of her academic and professional work. Beyond her literary career, Kuang has made significant contributions as a scholar and translator. Her deep engagement with historical research and language studies has influenced both her writing and her academic pursuits.

Education

Kuang attended Georgetown University, where she studied History, focusing on Chinese studies and 20th-century conflicts. Her research delved into topics such as the Sino-Japanese War and the legacy of colonialism in East Asia. She also spent time studying abroad in Beijing and Cambridge, further expanding her expertise in Chinese history and language.

After completing her undergraduate degree, Kuang pursued further education at some of the world’s most prestigious institutions. She earned a Master’s in Chinese Studies from the University of Cambridge and later completed another Master’s in Contemporary Chinese Studies at the University of Oxford. Her academic work explored themes of linguistic imperialism, translation studies, and historical memory.

Continuing her scholarly journey, Kuang later enrolled in a PhD program in East Asian Languages and Literatures at Yale University. Her research in this field continues to build upon her deep knowledge of historical narratives, language politics, and the power of storytelling. Kuang’s academic background plays a major role in shaping her perspectives, making her not only a celebrated author but also a respected scholar in historical and literary studies.
